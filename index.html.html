Article I follows
Preamble

Constitutional theory found me before I went looking for it, and most of what follows is downstream of what happened after that. This page isn't a resume. It's closer to an account of how I seem to think — as best I can tell, and as it's been reflected back to me.

ART. I Origin ratified

On Randomness

How it started, not how it was earned

I found constitutional theory by accident, not ambition. Most of what people call passion starts closer to luck than to virtue — mine included, and I'd rather say that plainly than let anyone assume otherwise.

ART. II Attention ratified

On Obsession

What happens once something catches

When a subject takes hold of me, I don't let it go easily. I'll follow a question into a rabbit hole most people abandon after an afternoon, and stay until I actually understand its shape.

ART. III Method ratified

On Precision

Less effort, better aimed

I trust the smallest correct move over a hundred generic ones. It shows up in research, and apparently in advice too — a plan I gave a friend off the cuff once matched a paid consultant's, point for point.

ART. IV Mind ratified

On Structure

An instinct, not a choice

I organize without deciding to — chronology, hierarchy, the logic sitting underneath a set of facts. Seven Supreme Court cases, unprompted, came out oldest to newest. I don't know how to do it any other way.

ART. V Weight ratified

On Fairness

Knowing what wasn't earned

I know what I have isn't evenly distributed, and that most of it was never really a choice. That's not false modesty — it's an accurate account, and it's why I think about the people doing the same work without the same luck.
